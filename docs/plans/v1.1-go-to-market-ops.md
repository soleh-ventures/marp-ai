## Q1 — Production WhatsApp sender migration

### Current state

- Twilio Sandbox sender (`whatsapp:+14155238886`)
- Every test runner has to "join" the sandbox by texting a unique code first
- Sandbox is rate-limited, marked as a test environment, and not usable for real users

### What "production" actually requires

WhatsApp Business API access goes through one of two paths:

**Path A — Twilio production WhatsApp (recommended for v1.1)**
- Buy / port a phone number through Twilio
- Submit business profile through Twilio Console (legal name, address, website, business category, use case)
- Twilio submits to Meta for **WhatsApp Business verification**
- Approval typically 1–3 business days; rejection rate is low for legit fitness apps
- Once approved: phone number is your production sender; existing `src/services/twilio-send.ts` works unchanged
- WhatsApp Business display name + green checkmark appear in chat once verified

**Path B — Direct WhatsApp Cloud API (Meta)**
- Skip Twilio entirely, talk to Meta's Cloud API directly
- Cheaper at scale (no Twilio markup on conversation fees)
- Requires Meta Business Manager + Facebook Business Verification + WhatsApp Business Account
- Longer setup (weeks not days)
- We'd need to rewrite `src/services/twilio-send.ts` + the webhook handler against Meta's API

**Recommendation**: **Path A** for v1.1. Stay on Twilio. Re-evaluate Meta direct when we're at 500+ active runners and the Twilio markup starts to matter. Specific numbers below.

### Migration steps (Path A)

1. **Pick the number** (see Q2 below) — buy through Twilio Console or port a number you already own
2. **Create a WhatsApp Sender** in Twilio Console — Messaging → Senders → WhatsApp senders
3. **Submit business profile** — Twilio walks through Meta's form. Required:
   - Legal business name (or sole prop name)
   - Business address (must be real, verifiable)
   - Website (`docs/gtm/positioning.md` lives where? Need a landing page)
   - Business category ("Health & Fitness — Coaching")
   - Display name (what runners see — "MARP Coach" or "MARP")
4. **Twilio submits to Meta**; status visible in console
5. **Approval** in 1–3 business days for clean profiles; 5–10 days if Meta asks for clarification
6. **Update `TWILIO_PUBLIC_WEBHOOK_BASE` Twilio webhook setting** — point at the new production sender
7. **Update Railway env**: change `TWILIO_WHATSAPP_FROM` to the new `whatsapp:+E164`
8. **Smoke test from one phone**, then deprecate sandbox

### What blocks the migration today

- No landing page / website (Meta needs one for verification)
- No registered business entity? (sole prop is fine; just need a real address)
- Display name decision (taste — "MARP" vs "MARP Coach" vs "MARP — Running Companion")

## Q2 — Indonesian (+62) vs German (+49) number for EU market

This is the strategic question. The user already has an Indonesian number; buying a German one through Twilio costs ~€15/mo + ~€20 one-time setup.

### Critical pricing fact

**Meta charges WhatsApp Business fees based on the recipient's country, not the sender's country.**

So if MARP's number is +49 but the runner is in Indonesia, the conversation is billed at Indonesia rates. And vice versa. The sender country choice does NOT directly drive per-message cost.

What the sender country DOES drive:
- Trust signal in the runner's chat list
- Local-number perception ("EU operator" vs "foreign coach")
- Compliance posture story for GDPR-skeptical EU users
- Onboarding friction (some EU users hesitate to message foreign numbers even on WhatsApp)
- Twilio's own number rental fee (~€15/mo for DE, ~€8/mo for ID)

### The trust / friction layer

WhatsApp shows the verified business name + a green checkmark once approved by Meta. The country code is still visible if a runner taps the profile, but the day-to-day chat experience is brand-led, not number-led.

**For an EU market target**:
- A +49 number with green checkmark + "MARP Coach" name = clean, no friction
- A +62 number with green checkmark + "MARP Coach" name = clean-ish, but a curious runner who taps the profile sees Indonesia. Some won't care; some will pause.

**Indonesia-first or Indonesia-also**:
- A +62 number is the *correct* choice for an Indonesian-first launch
- For a runner in the EU, the +62 still works (WhatsApp is country-agnostic) but feels off

### The compliance / story layer

GDPR posture is about *where the data is processed*, not where the phone number lives. Our Railway-hosted Postgres can be set to an EU region; that's the load-bearing fact.

But marketing-wise, "EU operator with EU sender number" is a cleaner story for EU users than "Indonesian operator processing data in the EU."

### The cost layer

| Item | +62 (Indonesia) | +49 (Germany) |
|---|---|---|
| Twilio number rental | ~€8/mo | ~€15/mo |
| One-time setup | €0 (you have it) | ~€20 |
| Per-conversation cost to EU runner | same (driven by recipient country) | same |
| Per-conversation cost to ID runner | same | same |
| Trust friction in EU | minor | none |
| Trust friction in ID | none | minor |

**Net annual cost delta**: ~€80–100/year for the German number. Trivial vs the trust upside if EU is the target.

### Recommendation

**Buy the German +49 number** if the v1.1 target market is EU. The €80-100/year is rounding error vs the friction tax of a foreign sender code on every runner's chat list.

**Keep the Indonesian +62** as the sender if:
- The market is genuinely Indonesia-first or global
- You want to validate cost economics on a cheaper number before committing to DE
- You plan to add a second sender (Indonesian for ID market, German for EU) later — common pattern, Twilio supports multiple senders per Meta Business account

**Compromise (cheapest validation path)**: keep +62 for the v1.1 beta (small audience, low signal cost), buy +49 when ramping public launch. The Meta business verification is per-number, so doing two numbers later means doing the verification twice — small ops cost.

This is a taste decision, surfaced at the gate.

## Q3 — Pricing strategy

### Unit cost model (per active runner per month)

Three cost legs. Numbers are estimates against current Anthropic + Twilio rates as of mid-2026.

#### Leg 1 — Anthropic LLM (with prompt caching on)

| Component | Calls/mo | Cost/call | Subtotal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classifier (Haiku, cached) | ~120 | ~$0.0001 | ~$0.012 |
| Domain runner (Sonnet, cached) | ~120 | ~$0.003 | ~$0.36 |
| Synthesizer (Sonnet, cached) | ~30 | ~$0.002 | ~$0.06 |
| Binder (Haiku, cached) | ~30 | ~$0.0003 | ~$0.009 |
| Flag-detector (Haiku, cached) | ~120 | ~$0.0003 | ~$0.036 |
| Block summariser (Sonnet, occasional) | ~0.25 | ~$0.005 | ~$0.001 |
| Plan generator (v1.1, Sonnet, occasional) | ~0.5 | ~$0.05 | ~$0.025 |
| Reminder templating (Haiku, daily) | ~30 | ~$0.0002 | ~$0.006 |

**LLM subtotal**: **~$0.50–$0.80 per runner per month**. Sonnet domain calls dominate. Caching cuts this roughly in half vs uncached.

#### Leg 2 — Twilio WhatsApp (recipient-country pricing)

WhatsApp pricing has three conversation categories that matter:
- **Service**: user-initiated, 24h window. Cheapest. ~€0.003–€0.005/conv in most markets.
- **Utility**: business-initiated transactional (reminders, confirmations). Middle tier. ~€0.02 (ID) to ~€0.07 (DE) per conversation.
- **Marketing**: business-initiated promotional. Expensive. ~€0.04 (ID) to ~€0.13 (DE) per conversation.

**Reminders are Utility-category**. This is the cost lever.

For a fully-engaged v1.1 runner:

| Conversation type | Per month | DE rate | DE cost | ID rate | ID cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Service (user-initiated chat) | ~30 (one-per-day-of-engagement) | €0.003 | €0.09 | €0.001 | €0.03 |
| Utility (1 reminder/day) | ~30 | €0.07 | €2.10 | €0.02 | €0.60 |
| Utility (2 reminders/day) | ~60 | €0.07 | €4.20 | €0.02 | €1.20 |
| Marketing (rare re-engagement) | ~1 | €0.13 | €0.13 | €0.04 | €0.04 |

**Twilio subtotal per EU runner with 1 reminder/day**: ~€2.30/mo (~$2.50)
**Twilio subtotal per EU runner with 2 reminders/day**: ~€4.40/mo (~$4.80)
**Twilio subtotal per ID runner with 1 reminder/day**: ~€0.70/mo (~$0.75)

#### Leg 3 — Twilio number rental + Railway + misc

- Number rental: ~€15/mo (DE) ÷ N runners on that sender
- Railway: ~$30/mo flat at v1.1 scale (one replica + managed Postgres)
- Misc (domain, monitoring): ~$10/mo

These are fixed costs. At 100 runners: $0.40/runner/mo amortised. At 1000: $0.04/runner/mo.

### Per-runner cost summary

| Market | Reminders | Total cost/runner/mo |
|---|---|---|
| EU runner, 0 reminders (free tier) | 0 | ~$0.70 |
| EU runner, 1 reminder/day (paid) | 1 | ~$3.20 |
| EU runner, 2 reminders/day (paid) | 2 | ~$5.50 |
| ID runner, 1 reminder/day (paid) | 1 | ~$1.50 |
| ID runner, 2 reminders/day (paid) | 2 | ~$2.00 |

This drives the pricing floor: for an EU paid runner with daily reminders, we need to charge **above ~$3.20/mo** just to break even on infrastructure.

### Pricing model options

**Option A — Freemium with feature gates (recommended)**

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | €0 | Chat coaching (rate-limited ~30 turns/mo), Strava connect, memory, GPX uploads, manual summarise |
| **Pro** | €9.99/mo | Unlimited chat, plan generator, 1 daily reminder, calendar export, priority response |
| **Premium** | €19/mo | Everything in Pro + 2 reminders/day, vision uploads (when shipped), unlimited replan |

Why this shape:
- Free tier validates product + grows funnel (cost ~$0.70/runner/mo, sustainable if conversion is decent)
- Pro at €9.99 yields ~70% gross margin on an EU runner with daily reminders
- Premium adds the 2-reminders-per-day delta plus future upgrade path

Indonesian market PPP-adjusted: €4 (Pro) / €8 (Premium). Still 60%+ margin given lower Twilio costs.

**Option B — Single tier with usage caps**

- One subscription: €12/mo
- Includes everything
- Caps: 200 messages/mo, 1 reminder/day, 1 plan per quarter

Simpler to communicate, but harder to upsell heavy users. Better if churn data later shows multi-tier confuses.

**Option C — Pay-per-cycle**

- €25 per race block (16 weeks)
- Then unlimited chat for the duration of the block
- Pay again when starting a new block

Aligns with how runners actually train (in blocks, not continuously). Predictable revenue per goal achieved. But unfamiliar pattern; harder to grow.

### Recommendation

**Option A (freemium tiered)** for v1.1 public launch. Free tier as the validation funnel, Pro at €9.99 as the workhorse, Premium at €19 for the small minority who want more.

**Indonesian pricing**: PPP-adjusted ~50% of EU prices. €4 / €8.

### Cost sensitivity to reminder cadence

This is worth flagging: defaulting to 2 reminders/day instead of 1 doubles the WhatsApp utility cost. For Pro tier in EU:
- 1 reminder/day: ~$3.20 cost on €9.99 revenue → 68% gross margin
- 2 reminders/day: ~$5.50 cost on €9.99 revenue → 45% gross margin

Strongly reinforces the v1.1-flow-redesign.md taste decision T3 — **default to one reminder per day**. Gives 23-point gross margin lift.

### Future: Meta direct migration

When to switch off Twilio to direct WhatsApp Cloud API:
- At ~500+ active paid runners
- Meta direct conversation pricing is the same (Meta sets the rate; both Twilio and direct pay Meta)
- BUT Twilio adds a ~$0.005/conversation Twilio fee on top
- At 500 runners × ~60 conversations/mo = 30,000 conversations/mo × $0.005 = $150/mo Twilio overhead
- That's the threshold where direct Meta starts to pay back the ~40 hr rewrite effort

Not in v1.1 scope. Capture in `TODO.md` for v2.

Three operational + GTM decisions for v1.1 launch readiness:

### Decisions made: 3 taste decisions, 0 user challenges

### Taste decisions (your call)

**G1 — Phone number: +49 German, +62 Indonesian, or both**
Recommendation: **+49 for EU public launch.** €15/mo cost is trivial vs trust signal. Use +62 only if the v1.1 target market is genuinely Indonesia-first or you want to validate beta on the existing number before paying for DE.

Recommendation: **PPP-adjusted at ~50% of EU prices** (€4 Pro / €8 Premium in IDR equivalent). Maintains 60%+ margin given Indonesia's lower WhatsApp utility rates.
Trade-off: lower revenue per ID runner, but matches what the market will bear. Could start with €6 Pro and adjust if conversion is low.
